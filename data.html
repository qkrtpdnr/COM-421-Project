Preparing Your Dataset

Begin by organizing your data in Excel. Use clear variable names, one observational unit per row, and avoid merging cells. Normalize categorical fields (for example, use 0/1 or Yes/No consistently). Check for missing values and verify that all data types are correct before saving.

Descriptive Statistics

Compute basic descriptive statistics such as mean, median, and standard deviation. Visualize your data through simple charts and histograms to spot trends or outliers.

Inferential Statistics

For inferential analysis, use SPSS or similar software to perform tests such as t-tests, ANOVA, or non-parametric tests (e.g., Kruskal–Wallis). Report the test statistic, degrees of freedom, and p-value clearly.

Presenting Data

When inserting figures or tables into your paper, always include a descriptive title and caption. Refer to each figure by number in the text, and ensure consistency between visuals and written discussion.
